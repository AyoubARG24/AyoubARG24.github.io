About Me

I am a first-year engineering student whose curiosity thrives at the crossroads of mathematics, physics, AI, economics, and geospatial sciences. From the elegance of Richard Feynman’s explanations to the revolutionary proofs of Terence Tao and Alain Aspect’s experiments on quantum entanglement, I see science not merely as knowledge, but as the *art of truth*.

Tools I work with include Python, LaTeX, AutoCAD, QGIS, and geodetic instruments. I believe every equation is a poem — Kalman filter formula \[ \hat{x}{k|k} = \hat{x}{k|k-1} + K_k \big(z_k - H_k \hat{x}_{k|k-1}\big) \] to Schrödinger's equation in quantum mechanics: \[ i\hbar\,\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\Psi(\mathbf{r},t) = -\dfrac{\hbar^2}{2m}\,\nabla^2 \Psi(\mathbf{r},t) + V(\mathbf{r},t)\,\Psi(\mathbf{r},t) \]

Research Interests

  • **Remote Sensing & Environmental Monitoring** — wildfire detection, land cover classification, and change detection.
  • **Geodesy & Ellipsoid Models** — precision positioning, GPS/GNSS, and mathematical modeling of Earth's shape.
  • **Mathematical Physics** — stochastic processes, Bernoulli percolation: \[ P_{\text{connected}} = 1 - (1-p)^n \] applied to fire propagation.
  • **Artificial Intelligence** — statistical physics approaches to AI, inspired by Geoffrey Hinton’s ideas.

Highlighted Projects

  • Forest Fire Detection & Tracking — NDVI and satellite time-series to detect and monitor fire spread in near real-time.
  • Percolation-based Fire Modeling — modeling wildfire spread as a stochastic network process using Bernoulli percolation theory.
  • PageRank & Random Walk Modeling — simulating ranking algorithms for social networks and information retrieval.
  • Geodetic Surveying — fieldwork with total stations, leveling, and drone photogrammetry for mapping and infrastructure projects.
